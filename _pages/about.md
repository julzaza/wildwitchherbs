  <div class="about-intro" style="margin-bottom:3.5rem;">
    <h1 style="font-size:1.8rem;margin-bottom:1.5rem;font-weight:400;letter-spacing:0.05em;text-transform:uppercase;color:#1a1a1a;">About Wild Witch Herbs</h1>
    <p style="font-size:1.15rem;line-height:1.7;color:#444;font-style:italic;">
      Where the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">green world</span> meets the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">unseen</span>. A space for <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">folk magic</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">ancestral remembering</span>, and the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">old ways</span> of working with <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">land</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">spirit</span>, and <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">self</span>.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="about-body" style="line-height:1.9;color:#333;font-size:1.05rem;">
    
    <p style="margin-bottom:1.5rem;">
      Wild Witch Herbs began as a personal <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">grimoire</span>—a place to collect the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">plant lore</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">folk practices</span>, and <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">spirit traditions</span> that have threaded through my life and <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">lineage</span>. Over time, it grew into something more public: a <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">living archive</span> for anyone drawn to the intersection of <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">herbalism</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">witchcraft</span>, and <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">ancestral wisdom</span>.
    </p>

    <p style="margin-bottom:1.5rem;">
      Here, you'll find <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">practical herb craft</span> alongside deeper explorations of <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">house spirits</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">lunar rites</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">Balkan folklore</span>, and the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">animist traditions</span> that teach us how to live in <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">relationship</span> with the world around us. This isn't about <span style="color:#999;font-style:italic;">dogma</span> or rigid tradition. It's about <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;font-style:italic;">remembering</span>—recovering the knowledge that lives in our <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">bones</span>, our <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">gardens</span>, and the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">stories</span> passed down through generations.
    </p>

    <h2 style="margin-top:3rem;margin-bottom:1.5rem;font-size:1.1rem;font-weight:400;letter-spacing:0.1em;text-transform:uppercase;color:#555;border-bottom:1px solid #ddd;padding-bottom:0.5rem;display:inline-block;">What You'll Find Here</h2>
    
    <ul style="margin:1.5rem 0 2.5rem 0;padding:0;list-style:none;text-align:center;">
      <li style="margin-bottom:1rem;"><span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">Plant Lore & Practical Herbalism</span><br><span style="color:#666;font-size:0.95rem;">Magical and medicinal uses of herbs, from protection and purification to fertility and dreamwork.</span></li>
      <li style="margin-bottom:1rem;"><span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">Folk Magic & Witchcraft</span><br><span style="color:#666;font-size:0.95rem;">Spells, rituals, and craft techniques rooted in tradition but adapted for contemporary practice.</span></li>
      <li style="margin-bottom:1rem;"><span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">Ancestral & Spirit Traditions</span><br><span style="color:#666;font-size:0.95rem;">House spirits, land guardians, lunar cycles, and the animist practices that connect us to place and lineage.</span></li>
      <li style="margin-bottom:1rem;"><span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">Living Traditions</span><br><span style="color:#666;font-size:0.95rem;">Research into regional folklore—particularly Balkan and Eastern European traditions.</span></li>
    </ul>

    <h2 style="margin-top:3rem;margin-bottom:1.5rem;font-size:1.1rem;font-weight:400;letter-spacing:0.1em;text-transform:uppercase;color:#555;border-bottom:1px solid #ddd;padding-bottom:0.5rem;display:inline-block;">A Note on Tradition & Ethics</h2>
    
    <p style="margin-bottom:1.5rem;">
      The practices shared here are drawn from <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">folk traditions</span>, <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">academic research</span>, and <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">personal experience</span>. I strive to honor the <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">sources</span> of this knowledge—whether that's a <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">centuries-old herbal manuscript</span>, a conversation with an <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">elder</span>, or the quiet teaching of a <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">plant</span> growing behind my house.
    </p>

    <p style="margin-bottom:1.5rem;">
      This work is inherently <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">syncretic</span>: it weaves together strands from multiple traditions because that is how <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">folk magic</span> has always <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;font-style:italic;">evolved</span>. At the same time, I believe in giving <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">credit</span>, respecting <span style="color:#3d5c3d;font-weight:600;">closed practices</span>, and never claiming authority over traditions that aren't mine to teach.
    </p>
